How We Work

AI in Our Consulting Practice

We use AI tools heavily in day-to-day work. Plenty of what used to need a team of specialists we can now do ourselves, faster. Below are some of the clearer examples, though the list is not exhaustive and the toolkit changes depending on the project.

01

Live dashboards instead of flowcharts

Traditional flowcharts are easy to draw and hard to use. Once a platform design hits real complexity, a static Miro page becomes a wall of boxes nobody can trace. For an IPP planning its German BESS expansion, we replaced that pattern with an interactive dashboard built in Google AI Studio: a navigable view of ancillary service flows, data maps, and asset-to-trade dependencies. The CTO and Platform Manager got a reference they could explore in real time, and the design handed over to implementation without the usual translation layer.

02

Working prototypes instead of mockups

On the same engagement, the trading desk needed an alerting framework covering every signal from asset telemetry through to trade execution. The conventional approach would have been a Figma spec, a UI agency, and a written specification document reviewed over several weeks. Instead, we used ChatGPT to build a working prototype of the interface, hosted it on a VM, and gave the CTO a live link to share internally. Stakeholders interacted with the prototype directly rather than reading about it, and the feedback loop with traders closed in a single working session.

03

Slides, faster

A good deck compresses strategy, market context, and the numbers that underpin both into something a CEO or investment committee can act on. Claude in PowerPoint takes over the mechanical part of slide building, so more of the engagement goes into the insight and less into fighting the template.

04

Agents doing the research

Every morning before 8am, local Claude Code agents run through tender portals, regulatory consultations, and trade press, and drop a one-page summary of anything relevant into our inbox. Clients tracking NESO consultations, EU REMIT updates, or looking for new tender opportunities get the benefit of this without having to run the process themselves.
